Fullscreen keyboard → navbar strip

On Android Chromium: tap Enter fullscreen, tap the field, then dismiss the keyboard with a quick tap up here. A strip appears at the bottom and stays until you touch again — the reported viewport is stale by a navigation-bar height, and 100dvh follows it.

The canvas behind the app matches this bar while windowed, so a normal strip blends in. In fullscreen it turns dark magenta: if you ever see that colour, you have met the bug.

Workaround: never keep fullscreen and the keyboard open at once — leave fullscreen on focus, re-enter on blur (authorised by the dismiss tap). With this on, the strip never appears.
Calibration: in fullscreen, type into the phone field and dismiss the keyboard. The app reads the OS navigation-bar height from the resulting viewport shrink and remembers it per orientation — that is the height env(keyboard-inset-height) omits, added back to the bar lift in overlays-content. navbar: not calibrated
